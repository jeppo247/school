# Khan Academy Efficacy Results: Evidence of Learning Gains at Scale

## Citation

Yamkovenko, B., & Bergstrom, A. (2024, November). Khan Academy efficacy results, November 2024. Khan Academy Blog. https://blog.khanacademy.org/khan-academy-efficacy-results-november-2024/

## Summary

This large-scale efficacy study examined the impact of Khan Academy Districts (KAD) and MAP Accelerator on mathematics learning for students in grades 3-8 during the 2022-23 school year. Using the nationally normed MAP Growth adaptive assessment as the outcome measure, the study compared actual student growth to NWEA's statistically expected growth targets. The study is notable for its inclusion of all students regardless of usage level, addressing a common criticism in edtech research that positive results merely reflect pre-existing motivation.

The cross-sectional analysis of approximately 350,000 students found that those meeting the recommended dosage threshold of 30+ minutes per week (18+ hours annually) achieved approximately 20% greater-than-expected learning gains, with an effect size of 0.36. However, only 9% of students reached this threshold, highlighting the critical challenge of sustained engagement. A separate longitudinal analysis of approximately 221,000 students with two or more years of data demonstrated a clear dose-response relationship between skills mastered and learning gains.

The study provides strong evidence for a mastery-based approach to mathematics instruction, showing that each additional skill practiced to "proficient or mastered" level yielded approximately 0.5 percentage points of additional learning gain. Students who increased their practice by 60 or more skills year-over-year saw typical gains of around 30 percentage points above expected growth.

## Key Findings

- **Primary effect**: 30+ min/week usage produced ~20% greater-than-expected learning gains (ES = 0.36, N ~= 350,000)
- **Dosage threshold**: 18+ hours annually (30+ min/week) required for full benefit; only 9% of students (~33,000) reached this level
- **Moderate usage**: 9-18 hours annually (15% of sample, ~55,000 students) still yielded ~7% greater-than-expected gains
- **Skill mastery relationship**: each additional skill practiced to proficient/mastered = ~0.5 percentage point gain
- **Longitudinal skill gains**: students increasing practice by 1-15 skills saw ~5 pp gains; 15-30 skills saw ~15 pp gains; 60+ skills saw ~30 pp gains
- **Longitudinal sample**: 52% (~115,000) of 221,000 tracked students increased skills learned year-over-year
- **Context**: an average 5th-grade student typically gains ~10 points on MAP Growth between fall and spring; a 130-skill course in 5th-grade math sets the scope of mastery opportunity

## Methodology

- **Design**: Quasi-experimental longitudinal study with cross-sectional and year-over-year analyses
- **Sample**: ~350,000 students (cross-sectional); ~221,000 students (longitudinal, 2+ years of data)
- **Grades**: 3-8
- **Subject**: Mathematics (primary focus)
- **Assessment**: MAP Growth (NWEA) -- nationally normed, computer-adaptive
- **Duration**: 2022-23 school year (cross-sectional); multi-year for longitudinal cohort
- **Products**: Khan Academy Districts (KAD) and MAP Accelerator
- **Comparison approach**: actual growth vs. NWEA expected growth norms; year-over-year self-comparison for longitudinal analysis
- **Dosage metrics**: Yearly Very Active Learners (YVAL) = 18+ hrs/year; Yearly Proficient Learners (YPL) = 60+ skills/year
- **Inclusion**: all students regardless of usage level (intent-to-treat-like framing)

## Relevance to Upwise

This study directly validates several core Upwise design decisions:

1. **Mastery learning works at scale**: the skill-by-skill mastery approach (proficient/mastered thresholds) produced measurable, dose-dependent gains across hundreds of thousands of students. Upwise's ACARA-aligned mastery progression follows this same model.

2. **Dosage matters -- 30 min/week is the target**: the 30-minute weekly threshold maps well to short, focused daily practice sessions (roughly 5-6 minutes per day). Upwise's session design should target this minimum while making it achievable through gamification and parent nudges.

3. **Engagement is the bottleneck, not efficacy**: only 9% of students hit the recommended dosage. This underscores why Upwise's themed gamification, streak mechanics, and parent-guided structure are not nice-to-haves but critical to unlocking the proven learning gains.

4. **Effect size of 0.36 is commercially meaningful**: this is a moderate effect size in educational research, comparable to moving a student from the 50th to approximately the 64th percentile. It supports Upwise's positioning around meaningful improvement without making direct NAPLAN claims.

5. **Year-over-year skill accumulation compounds**: the longitudinal data showing 30 pp gains for students who increase mastered skills by 60+ provides a compelling narrative for sustained subscription engagement.
