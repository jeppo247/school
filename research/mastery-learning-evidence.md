# Mastery Learning Research Evidence

## Foundational Research

### Bloom's 2 Sigma Problem (1984)
- **Author:** Benjamin Bloom
- **Finding:** Students receiving 1:1 tutoring with mastery learning performed 2 standard deviations above classroom peers — equivalent to outperforming ~98% of peers
- **Key insight:** Most students can reach high performance. The bottleneck is the delivery model, not student ability
- **Bloom's conclusion:** This was not scalable in traditional systems — exactly the gap AI aims to solve
- **Source:** Wikipedia, Education Next

### Kulik et al. Meta-Analysis (1990)
- Consistent positive effects across subjects and age groups
- Improved achievement, retention, and student confidence
- Published by Cambridge University Press & Assessment

### Bloom's Broader Thesis
- >90% of students can reach high achievement under mastery conditions

## Pre-AI Tutoring Systems

### Carnegie Learning Cognitive Tutor
- Randomised controlled trials demonstrated measurable gains in maths outcomes
- Effectiveness drops when students pushed forward without mastery or systems poorly implemented
- Reinforces: mastery is the mechanism, not just the tool

## Modern AI/Adaptive Learning (Emerging)

### Adaptive Learning Platforms
- Personalisation improves outcomes by dynamically adjusting content (ScienceDirect)

### AI Tutoring Literature Review (48 studies)
- Often better than traditional teaching, sometimes similar, occasionally no significant difference
- Strongest gains when AI paired with human support and high-quality implementation (NHSJS)

### GenAI Tutor Study (University)
- ~27% reduction in study time with maintained or improved outcomes (arXiv)

## Evidence Tiers

### Strongly Proven
- Mastery learning works
- Personalised tutoring works extremely well
- Feedback + iteration is critical

### Moderately Proven
- Computer-based tutors improve outcomes
- Adaptive systems outperform static instruction

### Still Emerging
- Whether AI fully replicates 1:1 tutoring (2-sigma level) at scale

## Positioning Guidance

### What we CAN say:
- "Decades of research, starting with Bloom, show that personalised mastery learning dramatically outperforms classroom instruction."
- "AI enables us to deliver that model at scale for the first time."

### What we should NOT overclaim:
- That AI already achieves 2-sigma outcomes universally
- That it always beats teachers

The evidence is still mixed and highly dependent on implementation quality.

## Bottom Line
- Bloom (1984) = foundational proof of concept
- Meta-analyses = validation of mastery learning
- Cognitive tutors = early scalable versions
- AI systems today = first credible attempt to fully solve the scaling problem
